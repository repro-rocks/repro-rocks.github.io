Why Repro.Rocks?

If you work in STEM in the 21st century then you're likely to have either first or second hand experience of at least one of the following:

{{emojify ":grimacing:"}} Painstakingly translating complex mathematical notation in a scientific manuscript into a computer program

{{emojify ":tired_face:"}} Spending hours trying to get code that was state-of-the-art 18 months ago to run because the supporting maths libraries have moved on or been made obsolete.

{{emojify ":scream:"}} Scouring the web for the dataset that the authors used for their experiment because the home page they linked in their paper was taken down when they moved institutions.

How Repro.Rocks can help you.

{{emojify ":construction_worker:"}} Make your work reproducible

If you are an academic or researcher publishing open source Machine Learning, Simulation or other compute code, find out how to make your work easily reproducible with our best practice guides.

{{emojify ":mag:"}} Find and reproduce other peoples' work

We curate a list of reproducible code and datasets, as well as community contributed 'polyfills' that make work even easier to reproduce.

{{emojify ":raised_hands:"}} Contribute reproducible works

Submit your own work or reproductions and modifications of other peoples' work for use by the wider community, contribute to best practice guidelines, answer questions in our discussion forum.
